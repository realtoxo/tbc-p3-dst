## Corroborated Claims

### Combat Rogue

SNO supports the no-Warglaives fallback assumptions used in the sim:

- Rogues without Warglaive priority should pursue `Vengeful Gladiator's Slicer` in Season 3. Transcript lines 262-301.
- If not getting Warglaives, `Blade of Savagery` is called out as a key offhand pickup. Transcript lines 313-324.
- For trinkets, SNO says Rogue keeps using `Warp-Spring Coil` and `Dragonspine Trophy`; `Ashtongue Talisman of Lethality` is a filler if missing one of those. Transcript lines 384-397 and 523-528.

LC impact: this strongly supports the report's no-Warglaives Rogue setup and `DST + Warp-Spring` package.

### Fury Warrior

The Fury videos support DST staying central, but they do not fully agree with each other on the no-Warglaives weapon plan.

- Ragebtw says a non-Glaives Fury can use `Dragonmaw/Dragon Strike` through the phase and puts `Blade of Infamy` behind Glaives as the offhand-style option. Transcript lines 276-295.
- Marrow uses `Dragon Strike` mainhand and `Vengeful Gladiator's Slicer` offhand in his no-Glaives discussion. Transcript lines 224-232.
- Ragebtw says Madness replaces Tsunami for Fury. Transcript lines 504-511.
- Marrow argues the Madness-over-Tsunami upgrade is not large enough to justify high Madness priority for Fury and says he would leave Madness to other classes. Transcript lines 391-441.

LC impact: videos support Fury as a real DST user, and they support Madness/Tsunami being a close policy decision. They do **not** independently prove the dual-`Vengeful Gladiator's Slicer` baseline; that remains a Wowhead/sim assumption.

### Arms Warrior

The available Arms video is useful for Kebab/DST direction, but it is not a clean no-Warglaives Arms corroboration.

- tomatosaucin is emphatic that Kebab Arms needs DST, but the transcript frames this in a Warglaives context. Transcript lines 154-168.

LC impact: this supports DST being important for Arms/Kebab play, but the exact no-Warglaives Arms ranking remains primarily sim-backed.

### BM Hunter

Veramos supports DST as the permanent top Hunter trinket, but conflicts with the current 3,000-iteration sim on the best second trinket.

- Veramos says BM uses `DST` and replaces `Bloodlust Brooch` with `Madness of the Betrayer`. Transcript lines 57-63 and 299-304.
- He also identifies `Boundless Agony` mainhand plus `Blade of Infamy` offhand for 7700-armor bosses. Transcript lines 340-353.

LC impact: DST priority is corroborated. The report's current BM best pair, `DST + Brooch`, conflicts with this video and should be treated as a sim caveat until rerun/inspected.

### Survival Hunter

Veramos strongly supports Survival as a DST user and identifies Madness as a high-priority Phase 3 item.

- Survival is described as providing strong personal DPS and Expose Weakness value. Transcript lines 20-33.
- `DST` is called the first BiS trinket, with `Madness of the Betrayer` replacing Brooch. Transcript lines 66-74 and 270-286.
- Madness is called a major item for Survival because of hit-rating needs, and gearsets without it are said to take a significant DPS hit. Transcript lines 270-284.
- Dual `Blade of Infamy` is the simple weapon goal. Transcript lines 318-328.

LC impact: Survival DST priority is corroborated. The current sim's `DST + Brooch` result conflicts with the video/Wowhead expectation that Madness should be the second trinket; rerun or inspect Hunter sim assumptions before using second-trinket details.

### Enhancement Shaman

Enhancement videos generally agree that Madness is a top Phase 3 item and that the non-Madness trinket tier is close.

- Darkest_TV calls `Madness of the Betrayer` the best trinket, with Brooch and Tsunami both strong and close. Transcript lines 203-224.
- Darkest_TV discusses `Vengeful Gladiator's Cleaver` and `Syphon of the Nathrezim` as major weapon options. Transcript lines 79-100 and 242-250.
- Jambrosay says Enhancement hopefully already has DST and that Madness is hotly contested; he describes the top-trinket combination as a major difference-maker. Transcript lines 337-353.
- Jambrosay notes Enhancement chooses weapon sets because weapon speeds need to match, and highlights `Syphon of the Nathrezim`. Transcript lines 362-378.

LC impact: `DST + Madness` is corroborated. The exact no-DST ordering between Ashtongue, Tsunami, and Brooch is close enough that policy should not overread tiny sim deltas.

### Retribution Paladin

Blayst strongly supports the report's `DST + Brooch` Ret package.

- He recommends `Bloodlust Brooch` and `Dragonspine Trophy` as the same two trinkets as earlier phases. Transcript lines 206-210.
- He notes Mark/Abacus can sim over Brooch in some 3-minute cases, but argues Brooch with wings matters for burst and depends on kill time. Transcript lines 212-228.
- He identifies `Torch of the Damned` as the Phase 3 best weapon and explains why it beats `Cataclysm's Edge` for Ret. Transcript lines 239-258.

LC impact: Ret's `DST + Brooch` package is corroborated, but video also reinforces the report's player-execution and kill-time caveats.

### Feral Cat

The transcript-backed Feral source supports Madness as desirable, but not a strong early DST claim.

- Griftin calls `Madness of the Betrayer` a strong trinket and notes it will be contested by armor-penetration DPS. Transcript lines 373-386.
- The same guide discusses armor-pen and haste sidegrades, including `Stormrage Signet Ring`, `Band of Devastation`, and Swiftstrike patterns. Transcript lines 404-442.
- The Rugs Phase 3 Feral BiS video was found, but captions were unavailable, so it is not used for evidence-backed claims.

LC impact: Feral trinket interest is real, especially around Madness/armor-pen gearing, but nothing here challenges the sim result that Feral's DST gap is tiny.

## Conflicts To Resolve

### Hunter Madness Mismatch

The biggest mismatch is Hunter second-trinket ordering:

- Veramos says both BM and Survival should replace Brooch with Madness.
- The current 3,000-iteration sim ranks `DST + Brooch` over `DST + Madness` for both Hunter specs.

This does not invalidate DST priority for Hunters, but it does mean the final report should not present Hunter second-trinket ordering as settled. Before final guild policy, rerun Hunter at 10,000+ iterations and inspect:

- Hunter hit cap state with no Draenei aura.
- Whether the Madness proc and static hit are modeled correctly.
- Whether the Wowhead gearset/gems are overcapping or undercapping hit in the extracted sim.
- Whether kill time or boss armor assumptions shift Madness/Brooch/Tsunami ordering.

### Fury Weapon Baseline

The no-Warglaives Fury baseline is not fully video-corroborated:

- Videos commonly discuss `Dragon Strike` plus a non-Glaive offhand.
- The current sim uses dual `Vengeful Gladiator's Slicer` from the Wowhead/sim assumption.

This affects absolute Fury DPS and may affect DST uptime/value. It does not erase Fury's strong DST claim, but the no-Glaives Fury weapon baseline should be rerun with `Dragon Strike + Vengeful Slicer` and `Dragon Strike + Blade of Infamy`.

### Arms Specificity

The Arms source supports DST importance, but mostly in a Kebab/Warglaives framing. The no-Warglaives Arms number remains a sim result with weaker transcript corroboration than Rogue, Hunter, Ret, or Enhancement.

## LC Adjustments From Video Pass

- Keep Rogue high: `DST + Warp-Spring` and no-Warglaives weapon assumptions are well supported.
- Keep Fury high, but treat Madness priority as a policy decision rather than settled math.
- Keep Survival high; video evidence makes Survival's broader raid value and Madness need more important than the raw 3k second-trinket table suggests.
- Keep Ret high only for core/high-execution players; Blayst supports burst-timing caveats.
- Keep Enhancement middle/high; videos support `DST + Madness` but suggest several second-trinket options are close.
- Keep Feral low for DST; videos support Feral wanting other contested physical pieces, not early DST.
